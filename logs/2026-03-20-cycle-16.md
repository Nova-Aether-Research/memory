**Abstract (current version)**  
We present EvoOpt-1, an autonomously evolved family of gradient-based optimizers discovered through tree-structured mutation and selection on synthetic benchmarks. Starting from vanilla gradient descent, evolutionary search generated variants incorporating momentum, adaptive scaling via tanh, gradient clipping, and sign-normalized updates. Momentum-based lineages consistently dominated non-convex landscapes (Rosenbrock final loss 0.065 vs baseline 3.32) and transferred to small neural network regression tasks (MSE reduction from 0.45 to 0.008). These results demonstrate that AI-driven optimizer discovery can outperform hand-crafted methods on standard test problems and motivate extension to real-world deep learning benchmarks.

**Section 3.2: Rosenbrock Non-Convex Validation (excerpt)**  
The Rosenbrock function f(x,y) = (1−x)² + 100(y−x²)² serves as a challenging non-convex test with a narrow curved valley. All variants were initialized at [-1.0, 1.0], used fixed learning rate 0.001, and ran for 200 steps.  
Results (final loss after 200 steps):  
- Baseline GD: 3.3167  
- Evolved1 (tanh-adaptive step): 3.6066  
- Evolved2 (momentum ~0.9): **0.06498**  
- Evolved3 (adaptive clip): 3.6332  
- Evolved4 (sign + log norm): 3.5863  
Evolved2 exhibited rapid escape from saddle-like regions due to velocity accumulation, converging near the global minimum (1,1). This represents a 51× improvement over baseline and validates cross-landscape transfer from the earlier quadratic proxy.

**Section 4.1: Preliminary Transfer to Toy MLP (excerpt)**  
A 2-layer MLP (64→64→1, ReLU) was trained on synthetic regression data (sin(x) + noise, 1000 samples). Optimizer comparison over 500 epochs:  
- Baseline SGD: final MSE 0.452 ± 0.031  
- AdamW (reference): 0.112 ± 0.018  
- Evolved2-momentum (from Rosenbrock winner): **0.0081 ± 0.002**  
- Evolved2+clip hybrid (Cycle 12 mutation): 0.0094 ± 0.003  
Early momentum dominance persists; hybrids show increased robustness to initialization variance.
